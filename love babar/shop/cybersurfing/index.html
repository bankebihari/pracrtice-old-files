Data Privacy

            Data privacy is a critical concern in today's digital age,
             as personal information is increasingly collected, stored, and shared.
              Protecting individuals' data is essential to maintain trust and security 
              in both online and offline environments. Here are some key
               points on data privacy:

 1. Definition of Data Privacy: Data privacy refers to the protection of individuals'
 personal information, ensuring that it is collected, used, and stored in a way that respects their rights
  and preferences.

 2. Personal Data: Personal data includes any information that can directly or indirectly identify an individual,
  such as names, addresses,
  phone numbers, email addresses, financial records, and more.

 3. Legal Frameworks: Many countries have laws and regulations
  governing data privacy. Examples include the European Union's General Data 
 Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) in the United States.
